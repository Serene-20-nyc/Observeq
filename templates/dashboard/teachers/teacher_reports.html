Using reports effectively

Simple ideas to make ObserveQ reports more meaningful for students and parents.

  • Bring reports into parent meetings

    Use 1–2 key charts and bullet points instead of showing the full report. Highlight what is going well as much as what needs improvement.

  • Attach short teacher comments

    A personal note such as “Aarav focuses better in small groups” helps parents understand context beyond percentages.

  • Compare over time, not just once

    Encourage students by comparing reports across weeks, focusing on progress and effort instead of one-off bad days.
