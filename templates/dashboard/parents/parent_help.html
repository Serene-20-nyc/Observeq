2. Understanding behaviour scores

ObserveQ converts classroom observations into simple indicators:

  • Green – on-task, positive participation.
  • Yellow – mild distractions or restless moments.
  • Red – frequent disruption or strong concern.

Look for patterns across weeks, not one single red session.

3. How to use this data at home

ObserveQ is meant to support calm, constructive conversations at home:

  • Start with what your child is doing well.
  • Ask open questions: “What makes this class easier to focus in?”
  • Agree on one small habit to try each week.

4. When to contact a teacher or counsellor

Reach out if you notice:

  • Red or yellow sessions appearing frequently in a subject.
  • Sudden change in behaviour over a short period.
  • Differences between home behaviour and classroom behaviour.

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to the most common parent questions about ObserveQ.

How often is my child’s data updated?

It depends on your school’s settings, but typically: subject teachers update observations after lessons, and the dashboard refreshes at least once every day. The “Last sync” time at the top of this page shows the most recent update.

Are behaviour scores the same as academic grades?

No. Behaviour indicators focus on attention, participation, and conduct, not marks. They’re meant to help you and teachers understand how your child learns in class, independent of test scores or homework marks.

Who can see my child’s information?

Access is limited to authorised staff at your child’s school (such as class teachers, subject teachers, counsellors, and relevant administrators), and to the parent/guardian accounts linked to your child in the school system.

What should I do if I think a report is inaccurate?

Start by sending a respectful message from the Messages section to the teacher who created the report. Share what you’re seeing at home, and ask for examples from the classroom. Most issues can be resolved through a short conversation and a shared action plan.

Can I download a full term report?

Yes. Go to the Reports & Downloads section in your parent dashboard. You can usually export a PDF summary for a selected term, including behaviour trends, attendance highlights, and teacher notes.
